Introduction

The purpose of this website is to introduce Helen Foster Snow and her legacy to the modern world. I have included an animation visualizing her travels, a simplified timeline of her life, and hope to later finish developing an interactive map with photographs taken from the HBLL's "Helen Foster Snow Papers." My primary motivation was to simplify the story presented in her own account of her life found in My China Years. Helen published the memoir more than 40 years after returning from China, and as was only natural, added in many of her own contemporary thoughts as well as details about the future lives of the Chinese people she met and worked with. While this undoubtably enrichens our understanding of her life and legacy, it complicates the timeline of the story she shares. I hope that the animations and other features included in this site are able to communicate Helen's legacy in an understandable and respectful way.

Timeline

Helen's nearly spent a decade serving the people of China - introducing Red China to the West, stoking the Student Movements, and founding the INDUSCO movement. This Timeline will highlight some of her greatest contributions and travels between the years of 1931 and 1940.

Departure to Shanghai - 1931

Helen entered Shanghai in the August of 1931 aboard the S.S. President Lincoln. Upon arrival, Helen took up residence at the famed Astor House - as "was in the American Tradition" (My China Years, 21). She was lucky to arrive before the disastrous Japanese invasion of 1932 as she was able to personally witness the beautiful foreign-owned Bund. Although Helen would grow frustrated with many of the other foreigners residing in the concessions, there was a certain Edgar that caught her eye.

Wedding in Tokyo - 1932

After over a year of courting, Edgar Snow and Helen Foster were married at the American Embassy in Tokyo on Christmas Day in 1932. They traveled to Japan for the wedding because Helen didn't "want to be married in a filthy, dirty place like Shanghai"(My China Years, 74). They honeymooned in Japan, Borneo, Malaysia, Hong Kong, and Macao before briefly returning to Shanghai before their exodus to Beijing. They also visited the city of Tianjin before settling in their new home in Beijing.

A New Home in Beijing - 1933

The newlyweds arrived to Beijing from Tianjin via train. They initially stayed in a bridal suite at the classy French Grand Hotelde Pekin and later moved into a rental at rented a house at No. 21 Mei Cha Hudong. After the honeymoon phase ended, the Snows relationship blossomed into a true partnership, motivating each other with their work and publications. Helen seemed to love their time in Beijing, recounting stories of cocktail parties, studying Chinese, and taking walks on the many "walls within walls" (My China Years, 89). It was also in Beijing where the Snows hosted artists, students, and other controversial figures in their home.

Student Movement - 1935

While there was something of a precedent of Student mobilizing against the Chinese government, Helen was instrumental in lighting the flame that was the December 1935 Student Movement. Helen not only hosted the Yanjing University's student government in her home, she instructed them on "propaganda" and consulted on their plans. Balancing her role as a journalist with her passionate activism, she took pictures of the protest from the sidelines. The Student Movement Helen consulted lit a wildfire across China and likely contributed to the infamous Young Marshall's drastic ideological change.
